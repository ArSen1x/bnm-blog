The first thing I learned during onboarding is that a README is a promise, not a guarantee. Half the setup steps were out of date, and I spent most of day one just getting the app to run locally.

## What I actually did

- Paired with a senior engineer to trace a request end to end
- Fixed my first "good first issue": a mislabeled button
- Broke the local database. Twice.

> The point of week one isn't to ship. It's to build a map of the system in your head.

## The moment it clicked

On day three, I finally understood how the auth flow worked — not from the docs, but from adding a `console.log` in the exact right place. Sometimes the fastest way to understand a system is to poke it and watch what happens.

Next week: my first real ticket.
