Early on, my questions were basically "this doesn't work, help." I got vague answers because I was asking vague questions.

## The template that fixed it

1. **What I'm trying to do** — the goal, not the code
2. **What I expected** to happen
3. **What actually happened** — exact error, not paraphrased
4. **What I already tried**

Writing this out solved the problem *before I asked* maybe a third of the time. That's the rubber-duck effect, and it's real.

## Why it matters for an intern

Good questions respect other people's time, and they signal that you did the work first. That reputation compounds faster than any single fix.
